Our History

Peach Garden Pavilion was founded in 1985 by two passionate culinary experts, Mei Ling and Wei Zhang, who envisioned a place where people could experience authentic Chinese cuisine in a warm and inviting atmosphere. With roots tracing back to ancient culinary traditions, they brought their family's secret recipes and a commitment to quality and excellence. Over the years, the restaurant has become a beloved establishment in the community, known for its exquisite dishes, exceptional service, and dedication to preserving the rich heritage of Chinese gastronomy.

Our Signature Dishes

Beggar's Chicken (叫花鸡)

A traditional Chinese dish where a whole chicken is marinated, wrapped in lotus leaves, and baked in clay, resulting in incredibly tender and flavorful meat.

Buddha Jumps Over the Wall (佛跳墙)

A luxurious Fujian soup made with an array of high-quality ingredients like abalone, sea cucumber, and shark fin, simmered to create a deeply rich and aromatic broth.

Dongpo Pork (东坡肉)

A famous Hangzhou dish named after the poet Su Dongpo, featuring braised pork belly cooked to a melt-in-the-mouth texture with a rich, savory flavor.
